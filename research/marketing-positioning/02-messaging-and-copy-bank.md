## 1. Message hierarchy (lead → support → proof)

```
LEVEL 1 — THE HOOK (demystify): "See why your posts land — as you write them."
   ▼
LEVEL 2 — THE WEDGE (voice + data): "Grounded in your own best posts and how X
          actually ranks. So it sounds like you and reaches like your best."
   ▼
LEVEL 3 — THE FORM (assistant): "You write the post. We underline what drifts from
          your voice and what'll lose to the algorithm — and fix both in a click."
   ▼
LEVEL 4 — PROOF (show, don't claim): the live underline, the grounded 'why'
          (a reply ≈ 27× a like; your 'question hook' → 2.3×), the Voice Report.
   ▼
LEVEL 5 — TRUST (human + honest): "You keep the pen. Transparent pricing. Built for X."
```

**Rule:** a hero section should move Level 1 → 2 → 4 in three beats. Never lead with Level 3 (the form) or Level 5 (trust) — those are reasons to *believe*, not reasons to *care*.

## 3. Value props translated per ICP (use the right one per channel)

### PRIMARY — Build-in-Public Founder/Operator (IH, HN, #buildinpublic)
- **Lead:** *"You shipped. You posted. Crickets. The problem usually isn't your idea — it's that you can't see what the algorithm rewards or whether you sound like a person. Now you can, as you type."*
- **Job:** remove the blank page *and* stop sounding like a bot, without hours they don't have.
- **Reply-growth stage (smaller accounts):** *"Grow by replying — without the AI-reply spam that's getting accounts suspended. Every reply stays in your voice, and you see if it's worth sending."*
- **Distribution stage (larger accounts):** *"Stop guessing why one post hits and the next dies. The assistant reads each draft against your own winners."*

### SECONDARY — Scaling Ghostwriter / Agency (guru economy, seat loop)
- **Lead:** *"Stop voice drift across clients. Every draft is checked against that client's voice and that client's best posts — so nothing ships off-brand, and revision rounds shrink."*
- **Rule (from `05-icp.md`):** never "we automate your voice" (= "we automate your value"). Position against ChatGPT's drift-at-scale, not as "another X tool."
- **Hook:** *"$199/mo is 4–10% of one client's retainer — and it's the difference between a 4-client ceiling and a real agency."*

### CHANNEL — Creator-Who-Automates (Claude/MCP, r/ClaudeAI)
- **Lead the outcome, never the plumbing:** *"Wire your own agent to draft in your voice — with you in the loop and the algorithm read built in."* Never "an MCP server" (anchors to $0).

## 6. Feature copy (each = benefit headline + plain body + the "why" that proves depth)

**Live Voice Match**
> **It sounds like you — and it tells you when it doesn't.**
> As you write, a purple underline marks any phrase that drifts from how you actually sound, learned from your own posted tweets. Not a generic "tone" setting — *your* cadence. Hover to see why, accept to fix.

**Live Reach read (the demystifier)**
> **The algorithm, explained on every post.**
> Links demoted ~30–50%. No reply hook. Engagement-bait that X is now down-ranking. We flag each one *as you type* and tell you exactly why — citing how X actually ranks, where a reply counts ~27× a like. Stop learning the rules from folklore.

**Your patterns, not a viral corpus**
> **Built from your best posts, not everyone else's.**
> We extract what actually performs *for your account* — weighted by real engagement — and check every draft against it. That's why it sounds like you instead of like the same ten viral templates everyone else is running.

**You hold the pen (account safety)**
> **You write it. You ship it. Every time.**
> No autopilot, no posting as you, no AI-reply spam — the exact behavior X started suspending accounts for in 2026. Every suggestion is yours to accept or dismiss. The assistant coaches; you decide.

**Starting points (generation, demoted correctly)**
> **Blank page? Get a first line, not a finished fake.**
> Need a running start, seed the editor from a topic, an inspiration post, or a deep research pass. It drops *editable* text under the live assistant — a starting point you own and refine, never a card you rubber-stamp.

**The Voice Report (the proof artifact)**
> **See exactly where your voice comes from.**
> The patterns we check against, traced to the specific top posts they came from, with the engagement multiplier on each. The proof that "sounds like you" isn't a marketing word here — it's your data.

## 9. Landing page skeleton (section-by-section, with the job of each)

1. **Hero** — Demystify headline + the Level-2/3 subhead + `Start writing — free`. A loop of the live underline + hover "why" firing as someone types. *(Show the product doing the demystifying in the first second.)*
2. **The problem** — the two fogs, named. *"You can't see if you sound like you. You can't see why posts flop. So you guess."*
3. **The shift** — "You write. It reads the room." The protagonist reversal vs. AI ghostwriters, shown side by side.
4. **The demystifier** — the Reach read with a real grounded "why" card. This is the screenshot that sells.
5. **The wedge** — "Built from your best posts" + the Voice Report. The depth, demonstrated.
6. **Account safety** — "You hold the pen." The 2026 suspension context as a survival reason-to-buy.
7. **Surfaces** — works in the dashboard composer *and* in the X box you already use (extension).
8. **Pricing** — free tier that's genuinely useful; flat Pro for the live assistant; honest metering note. Transparency as a trust section, not fine print.
9. **For ghostwriters/agencies** — the drift-across-clients pitch + per-client proof CTA (gated).
10. **Close** — the manifesto line: *"You keep the pen. We hand you the map."* + `Start writing — free`.

## 12. Words we use / words we ban

**Use:** voice, sounds like you, the algorithm, reach, your patterns, your best posts, read (noun), legible, the why, you write / you hold the pen, on-voice, land (a post), coach, co-pilot, demystify, see, show.

**Ban:** generate (for the hero), automate, autopilot, 10×, viral (as a promise), AI-powered (as the lead value), effortless/effortlessly, supercharge, unleash, game-changer, revolutionary, mush/slop (in *our* product voice — name the enemy precisely, don't sling the word). "Ghostwriter" only when describing the *enemy* or the *secondary buyer*, never our product role.
