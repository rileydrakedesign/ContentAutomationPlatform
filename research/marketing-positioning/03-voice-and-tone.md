# Brand Voice & Tone

> **Purpose:** How the brand itself writes. This is unusually high-stakes for us: **a tool that promises to keep your writing from sounding like AI cannot have marketing that sounds like AI.** Our copy is the loudest product demo we ship. This doc makes the voice in `01-brand-mission-and-identity.md §6` operational.
> **Date:** 2026-06-25

---

## 1. The voice in one line

> **A sharp, generous operator who has grown real accounts, hates slop, and explains the game plainly — confident enough to be calm, honest enough to tell you when a post isn't ready.**

We write like the smartest person in the #buildinpublic replies who actually helps, not the guru selling a course. Plainspoken, specific, a little dry, never hyped.

---

## 2. The five voice principles (with do / don't)

**1. Declarative, not hyped.** State the truth flatly; let it carry. Confidence is shown by *calm*, not exclamation.
- ✅ "Links get demoted ~30–50%. Move it to a reply."
- ❌ "🚀 Supercharge your reach by AVOIDING the link penalty!!"

**2. Specific over superlative.** A real number or mechanism beats every adjective. Specificity *is* our credibility (and our moat — it's how we show instead of claim).
- ✅ "On X, a reply counts about 27× a like. That's why a reply hook matters more than a CTA."
- ❌ "Our advanced AI deeply understands engagement."

**3. Teach in every line.** We demystify by default — the reader should leave a touch smarter, able to verify what we said.
- ✅ "Engagement-bait ('comment YES below') used to work. X started down-ranking it. Here's the tell."
- ❌ "We optimize your posts for maximum engagement."

**4. The reader is the hero.** Sentences should make *them* more capable, not make *us* impressive. Second person, active, their outcome.
- ✅ "You'll see why a post will land before you publish it."
- ❌ "Agents For X leverages cutting-edge models to revolutionize your workflow."

**5. Honest, including about ourselves.** Name limits, explain costs, admit what we don't do. Candor is a wedge in a category of silent renewals.
- ✅ "A link post costs more credits — X bills link posts about 13× normal. We pass that through, we don't hide it."
- ❌ Burying pricing; "unlimited*"; vague "premium features."

## 3. Tone by surface (the constant voice, dialed per context)

| Surface | Dial | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Landing hero | confident, spare | one idea, big air, zero hype |
| Feature copy | teacherly, concrete | always include the grounded "why" |
| In-product suggestion cards | calm, brief, helpful | **the product's voice = the brand's voice**; never naggy or alarmist |
| Pricing page | radically plain | candor is the message |
| Onboarding | warm, encouraging | celebrate the "sounds like me" moment, don't oversell |
| Build-in-public / X | peer-to-peer, useful, dry | we're a builder talking to builders; teach, screenshot, never shill |
| Agency materials | respectful of craft | never imply we replace their skill; we protect it |
| Error/empty states | human, plainspoken | "Couldn't read that one — try again in a sec," not "Error 500" |

---

## 4. The in-product voice is sacred (special rule)

The suggestion cards, the "why" popovers, the score labels — **this is where the brand voice is proven or destroyed.** A live assistant that nags, scolds, or speaks in robotic flags would contradict everything the marketing promised.

- **Quiet by default.** An underline is enough; explanation only on hover. Don't shout.
- **Always explain, never command.** "Links are demoted here — want to move it to a reply?" not "ERROR: external link detected."
- **Respect the writer's call.** Everything is dismissible; "show fewer like this" is always one tap. We suggest; they decide.
- **Praise sparingly and specifically.** "This one's on-voice and the algorithm likes it" lands; a confetti blast doesn't.
- **Honor `optimization_authenticity`.** Users who dial toward authenticity get fewer algorithm nags — the voice adapts to how much coaching they want.

## 5. The anti-AI-slop checklist (run on any copy before it ships)

If a draft has these, it reads as the exact thing we're selling against. Cut them.

- [ ] Empty intensifiers: *supercharge, unleash, revolutionize, effortless, seamless, game-changer, next-level.*
- [ ] Hedge-everything corporate voice: *leverage, utilize, robust, solutions, empower, cutting-edge.*
- [ ] Triads-for-rhythm with no content: *"faster, smarter, better."*
- [ ] A claim with no mechanism behind it (every claim needs a *why* or a number).
- [ ] The brand as the hero instead of the user.
- [ ] Exclamation points doing the work real substance should do.
- [ ] Anything you couldn't picture a sharp, skeptical indie hacker *screenshotting because it was useful.*

> Gut test: **would this line survive being posted to Hacker News?** If it would get called out as marketing fluff, rewrite it until it's just *true and useful.*

## 6. Calibration examples (off-voice → on-voice)

- ❌ "Unleash the power of AI to create viral tweets effortlessly!"
  ✅ "Write your tweet. We'll show you, live, where it drifts from your voice and where it'll lose to the algorithm."

- ❌ "Our cutting-edge platform leverages advanced models to optimize engagement."
  ✅ "Every flag explains itself — citing how X actually ranks, and which of *your* posts the pattern came from."

- ❌ "10× your X growth on autopilot!"
  ✅ "You write every post. We make sure it sounds like you and that the algorithm will show it."

- ❌ "AI-powered voice matching for perfect tweets every time."
  ✅ "It learns your voice from your own posts — and tells you, mid-sentence, when you've drifted."

---

## 7. The one-sentence test for everything

Before anything ships, it should pass all three:
1. **Is it true?** (Could we show the receipt?)
2. **Is it useful?** (Did the reader get smarter or more capable?)
3. **Does it sound like a person who respects the reader** — not a machine selling to them?

If yes to all three, it's on-brand. If it needs an exclamation point to feel exciting, it isn't there yet.
